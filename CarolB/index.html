Why Carol for Council?

A True Leader

Growing up in Littleton I've seen a lot of changes and most of them have been good. But Littleton is facing a very different future – we are older and almost built out. While the future includes redevelopment, we strive to define what shape it will take. Yet how it will impact quality of life is an unwritten story.

Proven Results

✯Appointed by council to interview city manager candidates

✯Selected by city manager to interview Community Development Director finalists

✯Raised money to support neighbor’s lawsuit against the city regarding The Grove (The incredibly huge apartment complex in downtown)

✯Elected to Littleton Public School Board 1993-1997

✯Founding member of Littleton Preparatory Charter School

✯Blogging on www.littletonviews.com - a citizen’s view of Littleton including citizen minutes of council meeting since 2005

✯Arranges and organizes a monthly Community Conversation bringing citizens together with professionals, state and other elected officials to discuss issues such as:
Denver Water Executives- to discuss the rate increase
Littleton Police Chief- to discuss the opioid epidemic
County Assessor- to explain our recent property tax valuation notice

✯Testified before the State Board of Equalization and the Senate on Urban Renewal

Goals as Council Member:

✎ Improve transparency by making all financial transactions available online( Open Book is one option)

✎Restore ten minute Scheduled Public Appearances and return to the four-minute Unscheduled time period on council’s agenda

✎Return the city sponsored district meetings – citizens need to meet together and hear what each other has to say

Carol Pledges to:

✎Meet regularly with citizens to hear their concerns and respond to their questions

✎Strive to ensure citizen questions to council are answered, if citizens address council there needs to be a timely response

✎Keep community informed by posting all public council communications on my blog

Carol's Accomplishments

✔I have spear headed three citizen initiatives that were passed by a majority of the voters in Littleton as follows:
✔Eliminated secret council meetings except for legal matters and required by law

✔Allowing neighbors to object to certain rezoning's that im- pact their neighborhood

✔Require voter ratification of any urban renewal plan that uses eminent domain and/or the redirection of public mon- ey from schools, parks, city and county

✔Serves on the Zoning Steering Committee

About Carol

I have 2 kids and 3 grandkids (Eli, Olivia and Gabriel).  I love to garden (hate to see winter come), discussing world events with Eli, marveling at Gabriel’s endless curiosity, teaching Olivia how to sew and grateful that I lived next door to mom for the past 30 years enabling her to stay in her home until her passing last year.  But there’s always a silver lining – our grandkids will soon be our new next-door neighbors!  I am one lucky woman. I am the owner of a Littleton small business and when I can get away, Marty and I love to travel the world.
