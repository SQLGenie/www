Littleton in Review

I have worked with the city business development department, the property owner, the shopping center management, prospective businesses, and outside developers. And we hear there’s something in the works. The Grove, a development of more than 160 rental units in historic Littleton, across from the old Court House. That development has multiple zoning violations, and has caused a decrease in the property values of the historic homes across the street from it. One of my points as a candidate is that that kind of massive development can happen in ANY neighborhood, regardless of zoning. Our recent city council looked the other way while zoning laws were broken, and surrounding property values and quality of life plummeted.” On a different note, I recently spent several months helping a couple in their 90s successfully fight Arapahoe County’s attempt to remove Marilyn from their home and force her into a care facility. Someone had turned them in under elder protection laws, which stipulate that no one—not even the accused—can know who turned them or why. Some of us citizens went to court with them and protested to the county about this itself being elder abuse. After an incredible amount of work and attorney expense, the county dropped their charges, and Elwood and Marilyn Johnsen were allowed to return to their normal life together at home.”.
